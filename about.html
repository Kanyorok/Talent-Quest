About Us

“Where dreams and ambitions are encouraged.”

Who We Are:

TQS Academy is a Sports coaching Organization which provides an environment where children of ages 2-21 can enjoy top quality football, Rugby, Basketball, volleyball, hockey, Boxing ,karate, Swimming ,kids fun games and other developing sports coaching from experienced and well qualified coaches, in a safe, well structured environment within the school area and other development centers.

At TQS Football Academy, we strongly believe in improving players’ individual ability, his/hers tactical knowledge and his/hers overall

decision making. To do this we use a range of coaching techniques aimed at giving the players ownership of their own destiny and letting them learn to make decisions for themselves.

At TQS Football Academy we will tailor our sessions to suit the individual as well as the group needs/School, in order to ensure every player improves and benefits from his or her experience with TQS Football Academy.

All our football coaches are enhanced ,trained and Certified . They are qualified in Emergency First Aid and attend regular Safeguarding Children workshops. All are qualified football coaches, and FIFA Licensed.

The Worthy Pursuit

THE WORTHY PURSUIT INITIATIVE is a non-political, non-sectarian, voluntary Community based organization. It is a development community based organization that fosters unity, love, prosperity and continuity. The goal is to empower youths to transform their economy and environment through innovative and sustainable programs, relevant and customised trainings equal access to resources and community leadership.

Our History

Talent Quest Sports was founded in 2021 from the streets of kibera slums,which later spread its wings in the slums of Kanjeru,kawangware ,kikuyu and Kabete. with a vision of identifying and developing talent within the area. Through this and soccer as its main sport, a greater target of putting kids in schools and involving them in mentorship programs was born. This has been a massive success with a bigger obligation to serve the community that made us the prime obligation. We have won multiple trophies since 2021 across all categories. Most notably the East Africa Cup – u15 category. Our players have gone on to achieve greater heights as well. Micheal Wanyama currently based in Sweden and plays for the Kenya national team, Stephen and Suleiman - were part of Kasi Kasi FC, Moriah Magoma - Currently with Juventus Academy in Qatar and DavidAnWdaweru - earned an educational scholarship with Al Saad.


Born and bred in Kibera and Kawangware slums, John developed a passion for soccer from a very early age. This led him to come up with the Talent Quest Sports Academy in 2021. A program that started from the streets of Kibera and Kawangware Slums and grew its wings to the slams of Kanjeru,kabete and Kikuyu into the soccer academy it is now serving the kids and youths of Kiambu into their sports career development. The challenge of getting basic training equipment, soccer boots for kids and youth was so huge that nearly made him give up but with a will so strong, his desire never left, His passion never suffered but only grew stronger.

Talent Quest has evolved to be more than a soccer academy to a community based organization namely the worthy pursuit that support kids and youths in the slums to pursue education,community development,Creating awareness about drug and substance abuse among the youths while chasing their soccer dreams. Started out with kids in Kibera but expanded to Kawangware ,Kanjeru and Kabete . We have put over 50 kids in high schools on scholarships, 35 are being supported in primary schools and 2 in Universities. This has been a major step in the right direction. Now, we have sights on something bigger. Bigger than our existence. Something for a game that has given us so much. Our Coaching Philosophy

At TQS Football Academy we consider it is our primary responsibility to make sure that players have fun whilst developing as footballers, which should hopefully instil in them a long term love for the game and love for each other as it is all for sportsmanship. We do not measure the performance of coaches in wins and losses, but rather in what players learn in terms of technique, sportsmanship and fair play. Our managers, coaches and officials are charged with the responsibility of controlling their players at all times during a match or training and will lead and teach by example.

Our Values
To place the wellbeing and safety of each player above all else. The priority is to keep all individuals as safe as possible and to minimize the risks as far as is possible in a full contact
To encourage the progression of the ‘all-round’ person, not just in sport but to try and instill the benefits of good education and the importance of family and friends and positive interests away from the sports field.
To take pride in our coaching, to ensure players listen to what we are saying and that what we are saying is relevant, in order to make sure we are coaching correctly. We will continue to try and improve our coaching techniques and methods and search out new ideas that will benefit the people we coach.
To continue to practice that you can learn from anybody at any time regardless of age, gender, experience or ability and to accept that at no time will we have ‘learnt’ all there is to know.
To use our position as a role model to young players in a positive manner, to influence them not only in terms of their sporting lives but also in terms of their lives outside of the game to help maintain a healthy and positive way of life.
To respect and champion the rights of each individual.
To build an appropriate relationship with the individual based on openness, honestly, mutual trust and respect.
To always display high standards of behavior and appearance.
To ensure that taking part, whether it be coaching or playing, is fun and to never let winning take over as the sole objective.
To involve the players in the decision making process. The outcome is more positive if people are working toward a commonly agreed and targeted end goal, rather than an end goal that has been assigned solely by the coach.
To listen to each of the players and treat their comments and suggestions with respect.
